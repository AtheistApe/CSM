  \item Draw the vector $\pmb{v}$ going from the head of vector $\pmb{a}$ to the head of vector $\pmb{b}$. Using the head-to-tail definition of adding geometric vectors, write a vector equation using these three vectors, then solve the equation for vector $\pmb{v}$.
  \item Draw the following vectors on the $xy$ plane: $\pmb{a} + \pmb{v}$, $\pmb{a} + 0.5\pmb{v}$, $\pmb{a} - 0.5\pmb{v}$, $\pmb{a} - \pmb{v}$, $\pmb{a} + 2\pmb{v}$. What do you notice about the heads of all these vectors?
  \item As the number $t$ varies over all possible values, what can you say about the head of the vector $\pmb{w} = \pmb{a} + t\pmb{v}$?
  \item Generalizing, suppose $P_1(x_1,y_1)$ and $P_2(x_2,y_2)$ are two points on a line. Find a ``vector equation'' (as in the previous problem) of the line through these two points.
